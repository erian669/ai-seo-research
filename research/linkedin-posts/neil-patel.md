## Post Content
Have you ever wondered what makes content citable by AI?

Most people point to structure and using schema markup.

And although that helps, it's not the number 1 factor.

So what's the number one factor? Freshness!

Just check out the results.

---

## Key Insights
- **Freshness (Recency)** is the #1 factor for AI-citable content (~91%)
- Structured formatting still matters (~79%)
- Clarity of language is important (~43%)
- Traditional SEO signals are less impactful (~35%)
- Authority alone is not enough (~17%)

---

## What This Means
- AI models prioritize **recent, updated information**
- Older content—even if high authority—can be ignored
- Updating content regularly is critical for visibility in AI search

---

## My Take
This reinforces a major shift:

- SEO used to reward **authority + backlinks**
- AI search rewards **fresh + structured + clear content**

If you're building for AI visibility (GEO):
- Update content frequently
- Add timestamps (e.g., "Updated August 2026")
- Focus on clarity over keyword stuffing

**Bottom line:**  
→ If your content is outdated, AI will skip it—even if Google wouldn’t.
